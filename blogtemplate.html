If you're not familiar, when2meet.com is a simple web-based tool used to schedule group meetings. A group leader sends out a link, individuals fill out their availability, and the website displays the best meeting times in the form of a multi-shaded greenscale calendar, where darker greens means more people are available, and lighter greens mean fewer.

I recently built a tool that manipulates this shading aspect to create unique pixel art. In short, given an image and a when2meet link, the program pixelizes the image, converts it to grayscale, determines the darkness of each pixel, and sends a corresponding number of bots one-by-one to the link to recreate the image. For the dark parts of the image, all or most bots are 'available', and for the light parts, few are.
