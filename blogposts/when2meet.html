Art on when2meet.com

07/01/2020

www.when2meet.com is a simple web-based tool used to schedule meetings. A group leader sends out a link, individuals fill out their availability, and the website displays the most-available meeting times in the form of a multi-shaded green calendar, where darker greens means more people are available, and lighter greens mean fewer.

I thought it would be funny if I could make art on this website, similar to how people make art on github contribution histories.

Here's how it works: Given an image and a when2meet link, the program pixelizes the image, converts it to grayscale, determines the darkness of each pixel, and sends a corresponding number of bots one-by-one to the link to recreate the image. For the dark parts of the image, all or most bots are 'available', and for the light parts, few are.

An interesting challenge was how pixel art translated to when2meet. One square box on the schedule represents 1 hour on 1 day, but you can actually specify down to 15-minute increments. Essentially, I had 4 times the vertical resolution as horizontal. To prepare images accordingly, I just had to resize them to be 4 times as high as wide.

My original goal of minecraft creeper —a pretty basic pixel art image— turned out to be so easy that I moved on to more complicated images, This project was definitely a fun one, and due to how much I learned about python, web scraping, and script writing, you can't say it was entirely stupid and useless.
